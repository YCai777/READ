WHAT'S NEXT

Over 70% of respondents conduct more than 80% of their daily communication online. For this generation, digital communication is primary. Close relationships are sustained through screens.

Yet the tools remain thin. Online, people compensate with emoji choice, punctuation, and timing. Small signals carry emotional weight they were never designed to hold.

Connection persists. But interpretation requires more effort, and misreading accumulates.

Misunderstanding may not be a failure. It may be evidence that digital communication is still evolving. To read each other online requires rethinking how presence itself can be designed.
