COMPRESSED

When communication shifts to digital platforms, many unconscious cues disappear. Tone is flattened. Pauses are invisible. Physical proximity is erased.

What once felt immediate becomes delayed. What was layered becomes simplified. Communication is compressed into characters, bubbles, and icons. Richness becomes bandwidth.

Paper once held the body inside language. Handwriting revealed pressure, hesitation, speed. Ink could smudge. Margins could shift. The page kept evidence of presence—fragile, but traceable.

On a screen, that trace is gone. Characters appear identical regardless of emotional state. Language becomes information: clean, standardized, detachable from the body. And unlike paper, which is fragile but persistent, digital communication can disappear in an instant.

Material influences meaning. So does its absence.
